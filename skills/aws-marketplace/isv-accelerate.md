# AWS Marketplace ISV Accelerate and Partner Programs

## The most important thing to understand first

**ISV Accelerate is NOT required to sell on AWS Marketplace.**

You can list a product, publish it publicly, accept subscriptions, collect revenue, and run a successful Marketplace business without ever joining ISV Accelerate or any AWS partner program. Many successful Marketplace sellers never join.

ISV Accelerate is a **co-sell program** that makes AWS field sales reps financially motivated to recommend and sell your product alongside their AWS deals. It is valuable if you want the AWS sales force as a channel. It is not a prerequisite or a quality gate.

---

## What ISV Accelerate actually is

Without ISV Accelerate: Your product is listed on Marketplace. Customers can find it, subscribe, and pay. AWS field reps have no incentive to bring your product into their deals.

With ISV Accelerate: AWS Account Managers receive **quota retirement credit** when they co-sell your product to customers through Marketplace private offers. This means they actively pitch your product when it fits a customer's needs, because it counts toward their sales quota.

That's the core value proposition: turning the AWS field force into a sales channel.

Secondary benefits:
- Marketing Development Funds (MDF) — up to $25,000 for demand generation campaigns
- ISV Workload Migration Program — customer credits to migrate workloads to AWS
- Marketplace Private Offer Promotion Program — funding specific to Marketplace transactions
- Co-sell training, events, and strategic guidance from a Partner Development Representative

---

## Eligibility requirements

All of the following must be met simultaneously. The program is by **invitation only** — AWS monitors partner metrics and invites eligible partners. You cannot apply directly.

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Marketplace presence | At least 1 generally available product on Marketplace |
| APN membership | AWS Partner Network membership at **Validated Software Partner** tier or higher |
| ACE program | Active in APN Customer Engagements (ACE) program |
| Revenue | ≥ $2,000 in recognized AWS account revenue at enrollment |
| Deal volume | ≥ 5 launched opportunities (ACE or Marketplace Private Offers) in prior 12 months |
| Pipeline | ≥ 15 qualified opportunities in ACE in prior 12 months |
| Training | ≥ 1 team member completed "Co-Selling with AWS" learning module |
| Partner Business Plan | Confirmed with Partner Development Resource in prior 12 months |
| Field Ready Kit (FRK) | AWS-facing sales kit approved in Partner Central |
| Customer references | 2 publicly referenceable customers approved in Partner Central in prior 12 months |
| Payee Central | Amazon Payee Central account configured for disbursements |

Most first-time sellers do not meet these requirements at launch. Build toward them over 6-12 months of active selling.

## Foundational Technical Review (FTR)

FTR is a separate AWS Partner Network program, also not required to sell on Marketplace.

**What it is**: A validation of your product against a subset of the AWS Well-Architected Framework (security, reliability, operational excellence). AWS reviewers assess your architecture against a checklist.

**What they check**:
- IAM: no hardcoded credentials, least-privilege roles, no use of root account
- Encryption: data encrypted at rest and in transit, KMS usage
- Network: VPC configuration, security groups, no public exposure of sensitive services
- Logging and monitoring: CloudTrail, CloudWatch, structured logging
- Resilience: multi-AZ deployment, health checks, graceful degradation
- Incident response: runbooks, escalation paths, contact information

**How to pass**: Complete the self-assessment checklist in Partner Central, or provide a SOC 2 Type II audit or AWS Well-Architected Review as a waiver.

**Why it matters even though it's optional**:
- Required to be listed in the **AWS Partner Solutions Finder** (a separate discovery surface from Marketplace)
- Required to qualify for ISV Accelerate
- Increasingly expected by enterprise buyers as part of procurement due diligence
- Passing it surfaces real security gaps before a customer's security review does

**FTR vs Marketplace technical review**: These are different. Marketplace review is about submission compliance (CVE scan, Helm validation, policy compliance). FTR is about architectural quality. You can pass one and fail the other.

## AWS Partner Network (APN) tiers

For context on the ISV Accelerate requirements:

| Tier | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Registered | Sign up, no requirements |
| Validated Software Partner | Active Marketplace listing, meet basic revenue/customer thresholds |
| Differentiated Software Partner | Higher revenue, more customer references, additional training |

Validated Software Partner is the minimum tier for ISV Accelerate eligibility. You reach it by having an active Marketplace product and meeting baseline activity metrics over time.

## Practical path for a new seller

**Months 1-3**: List the product, get it to Public, start signing customers. Focus on the product, not partner programs.

**Months 3-6**: Once you have 2-5 paying customers, register in the AWS Partner Network (free). Start logging deals in ACE — even deals you sourced yourself, not just AWS-sourced. The deal volume metric is retroactive and every deal counts.

**Months 6-12**: Pursue the "Validated Software Partner" tier. Identify 2 customers willing to be public references. Assign one person to complete the "Co-Selling with AWS" module. Look into the FTR checklist to close any architectural gaps.

**Year 1-2**: If you've generated meaningful pipeline via Marketplace private offers, AWS will likely reach out about ISV Accelerate. If not, contact your Partner Development Representative directly.

---

## What to expect if/when you join ISV Accelerate

- You get a dedicated Partner Development Representative (PDR) — a real person who helps you navigate co-sell processes
- AWS Account Managers can log your product as part of their deal in the co-sell tool
- You start receiving inbound co-sell leads from AWS AMs working deals where your product fits
- The Marketplace private offer becomes the preferred close mechanism (AM gets quota credit, you get revenue)
- You'll be invited to AWS Partner events, QBRs, and co-sell enablement sessions

The program's value scales with how much you invest in it. Passive participants get little. Sellers who train their team on co-sell motions, respond to co-sell leads quickly, and maintain a strong FRK get the most deal flow.
